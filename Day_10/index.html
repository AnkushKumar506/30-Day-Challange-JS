Lord Shiva is one of the principal deities of Hinduism, known as "The Destroyer" within the Trimurti, the Hindu trinity that includes Brahma and Vishnu. He is often depicted as a yogi immersed in deep meditation, residing on Mount Kailash. Shiva is considered both a benevolent protector and a fearsome deity, symbolizing the cycle of creation, preservation, and destruction. Iconographically, Shiva is often shown with a third eye, symbolizing higher consciousness and inner vision. He is also depicted with a crescent moon on his head, the river Ganges flowing from his hair, and a serpent around his neck. His weapon is the trident (trishul), and his sacred animal is the bull, Nandi. Shiva is worshiped in various forms, including the Lingam, a symbolic representation of his energy and potential. He is a central figure in many Hindu texts, including the Puranas and the epic Mahabharata. Devotees celebrate festivals such as Maha Shivaratri in his honor, which involves fasting, night-long vigils, and offering prayers and rituals.
